## AI-Pass Flags
- **Two cooperating mistakes worth flagging.** (1) "Cooper's key result ([*Physical Review* 104, 1189])" — that PR citation is Cooper, L. N. "Bound electron pairs in a degenerate Fermi gas," Phys. Rev. 104, 1189 (1956). The book has the page right, year right, citation correct — but the title "Cooper pair" wasn't coined until later. Pedagogically fine. (2) Engel et al. 2007 "long-lived" claim should be noted as having been challenged on lifetime grounds; the book does this correctly.
- **One arithmetic check.** "S = -1/√2 × 4 = -2√2 ≈ 2.828" — correct.
- **Tsirelson bound name.** Both spellings are in use; the book uses "Tsirelson" consistently which matches modern English-language convention.
- **The "lasers ten-figure industry" line.** $20-25B is eleven figures (10¹⁰ = 10 billion). Worth tightening.
- **2015 loophole-free Bell tests.** Hensen Aug 2015, Giustina Nov 2015, Shalm Nov 2015 — three independent groups within months. The book's framing as "simultaneously" is accurate within "the same year."
- **NIST FIPS naming.** FIPS 203 is officially ML-KEM (Module-Lattice-based Key-Encapsulation Mechanism), derived from CRYSTALS-Kyber. Book's "CRYSTALS-Kyber" is correct as the algorithm-of-origin but the standard name is ML-KEM. Same for FIPS 204 (ML-DSA from Dilithium) and FIPS 205 (SLH-DSA from SPHINCS+). Worth a parenthetical clarification.
