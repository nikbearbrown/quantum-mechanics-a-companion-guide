## AI-Pass Flags
- **"By 1900, the best physicists in the world believed the subject was essentially finished."** This is the common Kelvin-Michelson myth. Kelvin's "two clouds" lecture is real (1900), but the broader "subject finished" claim is heavily contested by historians. The line is dramatic and lands a hook, but is historically slightly too pat. Consider hedging.
- **Light-quantum terminology**: Einstein 1905 did not use "photon." Lewis coined "photon" in 1926. The book occasionally uses "photon" anachronistically — pedagogically fine, historically loose. Worth a one-line footnote.
- **"At 1 m/s, λ = 6.6 × 10⁻³⁴ m — twenty orders of magnitude smaller than a proton."** Check: λ = h/p = 6.626e-34/(1·1) = 6.626e-34 m. Proton charge radius ≈ 0.84 fm = 8.4 × 10⁻¹⁶ m. Ratio ≈ 10⁻¹⁸ — that's eighteen orders, not twenty. Minor arithmetic check.
